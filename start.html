Getting started, without boiling the ocean

Spire doesn't need an AI governance rollout across all four business units on day one. Based on our research, here's the fastest path to a real, provable win.

1

Pick one business unit

Start with Federal or Weather & Climate — the two teams closest to production AI (SHIELD IDIQ work, AI-S2S) and the most audit-sensitive.

2

Map one workflow

Identify a single agent-touched workflow — e.g. an AI-S2S data query tool or a constellation tasking lookup — to bring under PBAC first.

3

Pilot PBAC + audit logging

Stand up policy-based access control and immutable audit logs for that one workflow — a concrete artifact for the next board cybersecurity briefing.

4

Expand with the dashboard

Use the analytics dashboard to show the pilot's usage and controls, then extend the same policy layer to Aviation and Space Services.

What we'll need from your team

From platform / engineering

  • Which internal tools/APIs the pilot agent needs (e.g. a Weather or Space Services endpoint)
  • Current AWS environment details for the pilot workload
  • A named engineering owner for the pilot (Boulder, Glasgow, or Munich teams welcome)

From security / compliance

  • Any export-control (ITAR/EAR) data-handling constraints for the chosen workflow
  • Audit log retention and reporting requirements for the Audit Committee
  • A point of contact aligned with the CTO's cybersecurity governance chain
